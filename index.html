Avoiding Poor Living Conditions: A Student’s Guide to Wellbeing & Academic Success in Australia

Key Challenges

The webpage explores the challenges current students face due to poor living conditions and provide practical recommendations for future students—from universities and government resources—to help you secure better housing and thrive in your studies. Your living situation shouldn’t hold you back—let’s find solutions together.

Tips for renting

  1. Inspect the place before you pay any money (and take photos of any damage).
  2. Read the contract before you sign it.
  3. Your agent/lessor lodges your rental bond with the RTA (Residential Tenancies Authority).
  4. Complete the conditional report once moved in and return it back to your agent/lessor.
  5. You'll need an Australian bank account to get your bond back.
  6. Pay your rent on time and get receipts.
  7. Keep the place clean, tidy and undamaged.
  8. Keep to the terms of your contract.
  9. Make sure you know your rights and responsibilities.

Essential Tips

1. Start Your Search Early

Begin looking at least 3-4 months before your arrival. Contact your university's international student office for housing resources and recommendations.

2. Understand the Rental Market

Research average prices, common lease terms, and tenant rights in your host country. Be wary of deals that seem too good to be true.

3. Verify Before Committing

Always visit the property in person or arrange a virtual tour. Never send money without verifying the landlord's identity and property ownership.
