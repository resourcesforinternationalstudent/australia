Avoiding Poor Living Conditions: A Student’s Guide to Wellbeing & Academic Success in Australia

The webpage explores the challenges current students face due to poor living conditions and provide practical recommendations including—from universities and government resources—to help you, future students, secure better housing and thrive in your studies. Your living situation shouldn’t hold you back—let’s find solutions together.

How do poor living arrangements—such as overcrowding, unstable housing, or lack of basic amenities, noise disturbance—affect students' wellbeing and academic performance?

An ABC News article (Daly, 2024) discusses the difficulties international students encounter in accessing housing, noting that International students caught in housing trap, between expensive private accommodation and overcrowded rentals.

Challenges faced by International Students

  1. Housing Affordability Crisis: A significant portion of students allocate over 50% of their income to rent.
  2. Mental Health Implications: Students in unstable housing report higher levels of stress and anxiety.
  3. Academic Performance: Disruptions from poor living conditions correlate with decreased academic success.

Tips to Avoid Poor Living Conditions

  1. Inspect properties before committing.
  2. Read the contract before you sign it.
  3. Your agent/lessor lodges your rental bond with the RTA (Residential Tenancies Authority).
  4. Complete the conditional report once moved in and return it back to your agent/lessor.
  5. You'll need an Australian bank account to get your bond back.
  6. Pay your rent on time and get receipts.
  7. Keep the place clean, tidy and undamaged.
  8. Keep to the terms of your contract.
  9. Make sure you know your rights and responsibilities.
  10. Share accommodation with trusted peers.

Overcrowding and Stress

Research links shared housing to chronic stress from overcrowding, noise, and conflicts (Bentley et al., 2020). One student reported in an interview, "Sometimes, I don’t feel like staying in the shared accommodation for a week". A housing checklist could help students:

  1. Identify red flags (e.g., occupant density, noise policies)
  2. Assess privacy and conflict-resolution protocols
  3. Avoid financially unsustainable arrangements

Sleep Quality as an Academic Performance Factor

Noise-related sleep deprivation impairs mood, focus, and learning (Scott et al., 2022). A checklist empowers students to:

  1. Verify quiet hours and house rules before signing leases
  2. Prioritize properties with soundproofing or private bedrooms
  3. Document landlord responsibilities for disturbances

Enhancing Student Wellbeing Through Housing: University Responsibility, Standards, and Support

The study conducted by peers (Brownfield et al., 2020) in Melbourne has found that "Among 18,584 students across 38 Australian public Universities 9% of domestic students and 7% of international students reported deferring their studies due to financial reasons and that 55% of part-time students would have preferred to study full-time if finances permitted. Remaining 29% of the interviewees deferred their studies was due to financial stress."

  1. International students arriving in a new place, facing difficulty adapting to the culture.
  2. Finding accommodation at a more reasonable fare (considering living conditions, commute to university, accessibility, etc.).
  3. Desperate attempts leading to stress, anxiety, depression, and other health conditions.
  4. Lacking concentration in studies due to concerns about inspections, financial conditions, etc.
  5. Approaching student offices at the university, seeking assistance for their conditions.
  6. Learning about government subsidies available to support student living conditions.

Impact of Inadequate Student Housing on University Reputation

How does poor student housing affect the university’s reputation and retention rates?

  1. Poor housing conditions lower student satisfaction, academic success, and retention rates.
  2. Negative student experiences can damage the university’s international reputation.
  3. Word-of-mouth and online reviews influence prospective student decisions.

University Initiatives to Provide Safe and Affordable Housing

What steps can universities take to assist students in finding safe and affordable housing?

  1. Establish support offices for verified housing listings and rental advice.
  2. Collaborate with local providers to offer affordable, subsidised accommodation.
  3. Launch online platforms and pre-arrival briefings for informed housing choices.
  4. Assign student ambassadors to guide peers through housing-related challenges.
  5. Student organizations support students in desperate housing situations, offering 24/7 assistance and essential wellness support.

Promoting and Enforcing Off-Campus Housing Standards

Should universities enforce minimum housing standards for off-campus accommodations?

  1. Partner with councils and landlords to enforce safety and hygiene standards.
  2. Create a “university-approved housing” program for off-campus properties.
  3. Conduct routine inspections and collect student feedback for continuous improvement.

Supporting Students Facing Financial Stress from High Rent Costs

How can universities support students who face financial hardship due to high rent costs?

  1. Provide emergency accommodation grants and rental relief schemes.
  2. Offer access to on-campus or homestay options at reduced rates.
  3. Run workshops on budgeting and connect students with part-time work.
  4. UWA Guild aid to support every student’s issue and stand with them till resolution.
  5. Advocate for government policies that promote affordable student housing.

Government Interventions in International Student Housing: Support, Regulation, and Affordability

  1. How can international students find support from the government in avoiding and overcoming poor living conditions in Australia?
    • Free Financial Counselling (Revive Financial, n.d.)
    • International Student Accommodation Subsidy (Department of Jobs, Tourism, Science and Innovation, 2024)
  2. What role should the government play in regulating student housing conditions?
    • Introduce minimum housing standards for student rentals.
    • Incentivise landlords
  3. How can policymakers address the rising cost of student rent in college towns?
    • Mandatory Inclusion of Student Needs in Local Housing Planning (Charles Darwin University, 2024).
