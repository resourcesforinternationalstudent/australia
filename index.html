Challenges faced by International Students

  1. Housing Affordability Crisis: A significant portion of students allocate over 50% of their income to rent.
  2. Mental Health Implications: Students in unstable housing report higher levels of stress and anxiety.
  3. Academic Performance: Disruptions from poor living conditions correlate with decreased academic success.

A Guide for Future Students

Tips for renting

  1. Read the contract before you sign it.
  2. Your agent/lessor lodges your rental bond with the RTA (Residential Tenancies Authority).
  3. Complete the conditional report once moved in and return it back to your agent/lessor.
  4. You'll need an Australian bank account to get your bond back.
  5. Pay your rent on time and get receipts.
  6. Keep the place clean, tidy and undamaged.
  7. Keep to the terms of your contract.
  8. Make sure you know your rights and responsibilities.

Impact of Inadequate Student Housing on University Reputation

How does poor student housing affect the university’s reputation and retention rates?

  1. Poor housing conditions lower student satisfaction, academic success, and retention rates.
  2. Negative student experiences can damage the university’s international reputation.
  3. Word-of-mouth and online reviews influence prospective student decisions.

University Initiatives to Provide Safe and Affordable Housing

What steps can universities take to assist students in finding safe and affordable housing?

  1. Establish support offices for verified housing listings and rental advice.
  2. Collaborate with local providers to offer affordable, subsidised accommodation.
  3. Launch online platforms and pre-arrival briefings for informed housing choices.
  4. Assign student ambassadors to guide peers through housing-related challenges.
  5. Student organizations support students in desperate housing situations, offering 24/7 assistance and essential wellness support.

Promoting and Enforcing Off-Campus Housing Standards

Should universities enforce minimum housing standards for off-campus accommodations?

  1. Partner with councils and landlords to enforce safety and hygiene standards.
  2. Create a “university-approved housing” program for off-campus properties.
  3. Conduct routine inspections and collect student feedback for continuous improvement.

Supporting Students Facing Financial Stress from High Rent Costs

How can universities support students who face financial hardship due to high rent costs?

  1. Provide emergency accommodation grants and rental relief schemes.
  2. Offer access to on-campus or homestay options at reduced rates.
  3. Run workshops on budgeting and connect students with part-time work.
  4. UWA Guild aid to support every student’s issue and stand with them till resolution.
  5. Advocate for government policies that promote affordable student housing.
